Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) is increasingly important as humans rely more heavily on AI predictions


Executive Summary



The focus of the Carnegie Mellon Visualization Group is to clarify findings and improve understanding of complex data. Through an independent study collaboration with Dr. Adam Perer and Dr. Jessica Hammer, my research project is “Explaining AI (XAI) with Games.”

This project is a type of game with a purpose (GWAP). One of the goals is to help humans learn about AI through selection of feature visualization. Feature visualization are how neural networks build understanding of images. This is how AI interprets objects, patterns, animals, etc. One of our research interests is understanding how humans select and interpret these visualizations. Through this research, I am expanding my background in conducting experiments, designing user centered tests and applying data collection and visualization techniques.

Through this research, we offer a novel approach for XAI, integrating XAI with games with a purpose (GWAPs). GWAPs are games developed for humans to play online and generate usable data as a byproduct of gameplay. Explanability is an appropriate and challenging topic to tackle with GWAP methods because it requires human evaluation at scale for chunkable tasks.

Details of the Game




Background Research

In this multiplayer game, the first player, the explainer, selects a source image (e.g. cat). The explainer then selects the top 4 features that they believe will lead the other players to guess the correct answer as quickly as possible.

The other players are guessers and will compete against each other to guess the source image.

Each player that is an explainer is also a guesser during the game where they try to identify what the source image based on feature visualizations other players choose.


Research Questions

1. Will players who are explainers identify feature visualizations that AI also select to best represent source images? Similarly, will guessers be able to interpret top-ranked explanations better than lower ranked explanations?

2. Should players improve at guessing source images during rounds or over the course of multiple games?

3. Can gamification with points for guessing attributes of feature visualization images improve guessing and user satisfaction when playing?

4. How might GWAP techniques in our research be generalized to other XAI problems?
