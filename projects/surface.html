Overview



My goal is to impact and empower people through technology; this includes designing for consumer electronics that allow people to create.

During my second summer internship at Microsoft, I worked on the Surface Hardware Team as a Mechanical Engineering, Product Design Intern.

I ensured quality of a new product working with engineers on the Mechanical Engineering and Industrial Design teams. I also engaged in international travel to assist a product build at manufacturing HQ and supplier sites in China.

Roles

During my summer internship, my roles included:

  • Performing geometric and tolerance analysis (GD&T) and simulations for the Surface Slim Pen (released Fall 2019) to accompany Surface Duo device (scheduled release date 'Expected Holidays 2020')
  • Communicating with the Mechanical Engineering and Industrial Design teams to coordinate function and design goals
  • Learning about an engineering validation (EV) build process and broadening understanding of Manufacturing and Metrology
  • Visiting manufacturing headquarters for Surface in China, near Shanghai, to assist with EV development
  • Delivering analyses and key insights to stakeholders


Design for the Surface Slim

Now that the Surface Slim is released, I can share some details of my work. The Slim is designed to accompany all Surface Devices, including the upcoming Duo and Neo Devices (announced Fall 2019, expected late 2020).

A focus for my internship was applying Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) Analysis to assess fit of modeled parts pre-production.

Dimensional Engineering (DE) is critical for ensuring better manufacturing specifications for parts, and can reduce costs in production. As part of my work, I focused on dimensional engineering for the Slims' tip, housing, and enclosure components.

I used PTC Creo, a professional Computer Aided Design (CAD) modeling software, for part fixture design, and advanced designs through statistical Variation Analysis (VA) by assessing parts' fit and modes of failure.

The models which I created informed design for internal components of the Surface Slim. I met regularly with members from Mechanical Design and Industrial Design teams to report progress and ensure that analyses impacted drawings for manufacturing appropriately.



I learned the importance of tolerance analysis for mechanical design best when I traveled to China to meet suppliers and assist with an engineering validation (EV) line bring up for the Surface Slim.

Contributing to //Oneweek Hackathon Team

During Microsoft's 2017 //OneWeek Hackathon, I offered product design support for 3D Printed Bionic Prosthesis. The arm was designed to cost $25 as a cost-effective, simple tool with gripping and release functionality. The arm was prototyped for child in Seattle who wanted assistance for tasks, e.g. riding a bike. Our team won Microsoft's Corporate Hackathon Hack4Good Challenge out of 489 projects.


Enjoying time with the ME Team in Redmond

I am grateful to the Microsoft Surface Mechanical Design team for the opportunity to learn during my summer 2017 internship, especially through my travels to China. I came to better understand how device design, prototyping, and testing lend themselves to creation of consumer electronics. It was incredible to work with talented designers, engineers, and product managers early in my career. Coming away from the internship, I felt motivated to pursue an advanced education, which may lead to working at the forefront of product development.
